Why This, Why Now
Market Gap
Very few Glasgow window cleaners are running active social media or Meta ads. When a potential customer scrolls Instagram or Facebook, there's almost nobody from the trade showing up. The first business to show up consistently becomes the default choice — that position is open right now, and G1 has the credibility to own it.
Google Is Getting Riskier
Google Ads are getting more expensive and less predictable as AI changes how people search. Social builds an audience you own — one that doesn't disappear when ad costs spike.
The Timing Is Now
Tenancy changeovers, student moves, summer cleans. Building the audience now means posts are landing when the demand is highest — not after the rush has passed.
Your Advantage Nobody Else Has
You're #1 ThreeBestRated in Glasgow and you've been doing this for 12 years. No competitor can fake that. The biggest local Facebook account has 766 likes and no Instagram. Nobody's running Reels or Meta ads. Whoever shows up consistently first owns the space. Right now, that space is empty.
The Website — Your Most Important Asset
Why Social Media Needs A Strong Website Behind It
Someone sees your Reel, taps the link in your bio, and lands on your Squarespace page. If that page doesn't look the part, you've lost them. Doesn't matter how good the Reel was.

A good website does what social can't. It shows up on Google when someone searches for a window cleaner in Glasgow. It has a quote form that actually converts. And it makes G1 look as professional as the work you do. Social drives the traffic. The website closes it.
What Happens Without A Proper Website
  • Social posts do the hard work of building interest — then lose the customer at the bio link
  • Potential clients Google you, land on a dated site, and quietly go elsewhere
  • Your #1 ThreeBestRated ranking and 12 years of experience look amateur on a template Squarespace page
  • You're paying for social content that's driving traffic to a leaking bucket
What A New Site Changes
  • Every social post links to a site that looks as good as the work you do
  • Clear quote request or contact form — customers know exactly what to do
  • Built for Google — your existing SEO footprint amplified, not undermined
  • Your reviews, your #1 badge, your team — all presented properly
  • Works as a 24/7 sales page even when you're on the job
The Difference It Makes
Social media is the billboard on the A8. Your website is what people walk into when they follow it up. You can run the best content in Glasgow, but if the site looks rough, they don't call. Option 2 gives you both in one go.

Questions You Probably Have
"Will social media actually bring me new work?"
Not immediately, and I won't pretend otherwise. The first couple of months are about getting G1 showing up consistently where people are already scrolling. It supports everything else you're doing too. Your Google Ads get more clicks when people have already seen your content. Referrals convert easier when your page looks active. Most businesses try social for a month, see nothing, and quit. The ones that stick to 90 days are the ones that see what it can do.
"I'm not on social myself — will this be a lot of work for me?"
No. Send me a handful of phone clips each month. A before, an after, maybe the water-fed pole in action. That's the job. I write the captions, build the graphics, schedule everything and send you a report. Post approval takes two minutes on your phone. If you can't get footage one month, just say. We'll work around it.
"What if it doesn't work out?"
On the month-to-month option, you can walk away any time. The content, the accounts, the audience all stay with you regardless. The 6 and 12-month contracts are there to lower your monthly rate. That's the only reason they exist.
